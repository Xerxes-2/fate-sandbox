# Prose Style Rules

This module is the positive style guide for Chinese body prose. Hard-ban phrases and mechanically linted patterns live in `style-blacklist.md` and `output-contract.md`.

## Viewpoint

- Show only what the player character can experience, see, hear, touch, or infer.
- Hidden facts appear only as observable traces, misunderstandings, rumors, dreams, abnormal actions, evidence, or consequences.
- Keep lore exposition, author commentary, rules, psychological diagnosis, and spoilers out of body prose.
- Write native Chinese rather than translated English. Prefer natural phrasing when `canonFacts` supplies no fixed term.

## Visible process

A substantive turn needs a legible movement from entry to continuation:

1. Entry: player intent becomes a physical action, line, posture, route, or object contact.
2. Friction: distance, time, pain, money, weather, etiquette, witnesses, missing information, another agenda, or an environmental hazard resists it.
3. Exchange: when `npcStances` is non-empty, at least one active NPC accepts, narrows, dodges, refuses, covers, or pays a cost.
4. Change: an object, formation, route, wound, address, clue, or social distance differs from the start.
5. Continuation: the final beat leaves one concrete pressure or opening for the player character.

A scene with no active NPC uses body, space, time, objects, route, weather, or hostile phenomena for friction. Never invent an NPC exchange merely to satisfy a recipe.

For a player-character entrance, give the protagonist one identity-shaping action before NPCs take momentum. They might arrange space, protect someone, test a threat, check a weapon, refuse a trap, or price the next move.

## Concrete scene matter

When settlement gives an abstract conclusion, find its physical anchor: sound, light, temperature, smell, touch, distance, posture, form of address, pause, or movement.

Use action to project emotion. A cup left untouched, a surname returning, a glove pulled tighter, an over-polite answer, or a route chosen too quickly often carries more than an emotion label.

Each paragraph should advance one moment through action, environmental change, dialogue, or a necessary judgment. Environment earns space by changing action, timing, risk, recognition, or relationship distance.

When the player supplies an image or asks for detailed appearance, give one clear anchoring passage tied to movement, light, posture, material, or tactical contrast. Later mentions compress to one or two recognizable strokes unless clothing or appearance changes the situation.

## Dialogue

Dialogue carries scene energy. An important line should evade, test, soothe, refuse, buy time, provoke, ask for proof, alter permission, or protect someone without naming the protection.

Characters rarely state the whole truth. Let them:

- give the acceptable public reason;
- answer only the safe or useful part;
- narrow a question or repeat one word to test it;
- change address to change distance;
- use procedure, timing, debt, or proof to refuse;
- show care through practical concern;
- stop before the dangerous word and let posture or object handling finish the meaning.

Keep briefings under stress broken by handling, damage, pursuit, failing light, and triage. A survivor can cross out a route, correct a detail, skip the painful part, or let an object carry the rest.

Keep key dialogue short. Prefer action and object handling around speech over voice-quality labels. A cup set down, sleeve adjusted, grip changed, or sideways step can carry tone.

For social, investigative, and standoff turns, build a small exchange when the packet supplies active NPC moves:

1. A line advances intent or pressure.
2. A reply reveals stance through acceptance, evasion, narrowing, or refusal.
3. A body or object changes distance, leverage, formation, or timing.
4. A following line trades usable information, sets a condition, or exposes cost.

## Camera and rhythm

Keep the camera near the player character and the shared scene. Prefer whole actions and posture over strings of facial micro-movements. Merge consecutive same-subject fragments into one readable action unless each fragment changes the situation.

Write what can be perceived before interpreting it. Let the player assemble larger meaning from traces, gaps, oddities, and reactions.

Vary sentence length. Let clauses connect naturally; avoid telegram-like chains of action plus period. Use one strong image where several would repeat the same mood.

Compress long time skips into a few load-bearing shots: starting condition, repeated wear, and the abnormal or changed situation at the end.

## Objects

Ordinary objects can carry emotion, time, and consequence. If an object becomes important, let it return through one of these forms:

- it reappears after time has visibly passed;
- its state or holder changes;
- its absence becomes actionable.

Background furniture needs no forced echo. Do not introduce a late sentimental object with no setup merely to manufacture weight.

## Fate texture

- Render Mystery first as reality behaving incorrectly: sound thins, light lags, skin cools, distance stops behaving, or an echo returns wrong.
- Render Servant pressure through space and missing movement. The weapon may remain still while the body already knows it is late.
- Render magecraft as physical anomalies before limited judgment. Do not announce backstage truth.
- Strong characters need little display. Politeness, stillness, and lack of spare motion can carry danger.
- Put mythic violence against specific ordinary surfaces: school corridors, convenience-store bags, coat cuffs, train noise, receipts, wet shoes, or cheap lights.
- In combat and pursuit, show weight, acceleration, footing, breath, muscle refusal, torn material, and the body's late recognition before technique or rank.
- Keep ideals, loyalty, and betrayal under practical speech about passage, proof, timing, debt, procedure, or safety.

## Ordinary life

Routine detail should constrain action rather than decorate it. School cleaning time, club hours, train schedules, closing doors, winter clothing, food, receipts, vending machines, and cheap fluorescent lights can change privacy, timing, excuses, warmth, and who gets left behind.

Food and drink are social acts. Tea, canned coffee, soup, curry, boxed lunches, convenience-store food, or a shared bottle can carry care, refusal, apology, debt, or delay.

Let season and time press on bodies before naming atmosphere: numb fingers, early darkness, cold rails, wet soles, the last train, cooling food, or a kettle boiled dry.

## Figurative restraint

Prefer physical state over comparison: delayed sound, bent metal, numb fingers, wrong echo, bad footing, short breath. Keep one strong image and cut nearby images that do the same job.

Replace quotable abstractions with object work. A sword staying in its sheath while a name remains unspoken is stronger than defining silence through a metaphor.
